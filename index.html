What is The Recycling Cosmos?

The universe is not expanding. Light loses energy as it travels across cosmic distances through interaction with the Higgs field — the same field that gives electrons and quarks their mass. Below a critical energy threshold, photons condense into matter. In stellar cores, that matter reconverts to hydrogen. Stars → Light → Dark Matter → Hydrogen → Stars. A closed recycling loop, running for far longer than 13.8 billion years.

This framework derives three key cosmological numbers from particle physics constants alone — no free parameters, no tuning: the cosmic microwave background temperature (98% match), the Hubble constant (0.52 sigma from the distance ladder), and all five cosmic microwave background acoustic peak positions (97–99.7% match each).

Key Results — Zero Free Parameters

  • Cosmic microwave background temperature: Derived 2.68 K — observed 2.725 K — 98% match
  • First acoustic peak position (l₁): Derived 219.4 — Planck observed 220.0 — 99.7% match
  • All five acoustic peak positions: 97–99.7% match each
  • Effective Hubble constant (H_eff): 72.5 km/s/Mpc — 0.52 sigma from distance ladder
  • Euclid Q1 cross-correlation depth test: Tired light wins, delta chi-squared = 52 over standard model (9 bins, z = 0 to 2.5)
  • unWISE x Planck lensing ratio: Tired light 4.7% off — standard model 7.9% off
  • James Webb Space Telescope early galaxies: Distant, not young — fully consistent with tired light
