What is The Recycling Cosmos?

The universe is not expanding. Light loses energy as it travels across cosmic distances, creating the illusion of acceleration. Below a critical energy threshold, photons condense into dark matter. In stellar cores, dark matter reconverts to hydrogen. Stars → Light → Dark Matter → Hydrogen → Stars. A closed recycling loop, steady-state for billions of years.

This framework replaces Big Bang inflation with a simpler, parameter-free model that matches observations with zero adjustments: the cosmic microwave background temperature, all five peak positions, the Hubble constant, and observations from the James Webb Space Telescope.

Key Results (Zero Free Parameters)

  • Cosmic microwave background temperature: 2.68 K (98% match, Planck observed 2.725 K)
  • First peak position (ℓ₁): 219.4 (Planck: 220.0, 99.7% match)
  • All five peak positions: Within 1-3% of Planck observations
  • Hubble constant (H_eff): 72.5 km/s/Mpc (0.52σ from distance ladder)
  • James Webb Space Telescope early galaxies: Distant, not young — consistent with tired light
